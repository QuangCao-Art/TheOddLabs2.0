# The Odd Labs: Story & Lore Progression

## The Mission
**The Odd Labs** was established to research the vast potential of cellular life. Scientists here dedicated their lives to engineering cells that could accelerate environmental healing and improve human health. For years, it was a place of hope and discovery.

## The Incident: "The Spiced Mutation"
Years ago, a mysterious "accident" occurred that changed everything. Overnight, the microscopic cells mutated into sentient, large-scale entities (reaching up to 15cm). While the staff and the public grew to love these helpful "Cells," **Director Capsain** turned against them, claiming they were unstable anomalies.

### The False Accident (The Cover-up)
To hide the truth, Director Capsain officially logged the event as **"The Ionization Leak of '27."** He claimed a faulty reactor shield allowed high-energy particles to bathe the petri dishes, causing "unforeseen genetic drift." This allowed him to blame aging equipment rather than his own actions.

### The True Plot Twist
The "Ionization Leak" never happened. While working late, Director Capsain accidentally dropped a generous amount of **"Inferno Chili Sauce"** into the central neutrophil immune cell dish while eating extra-spicy noodles.

In a state of pure panic, the Director watched as the first cell mutated into the **Origin Nitrophil**. Thinking it was the only one, he immediately hid it away in the Old Lab Room before anyone else arrived. In his haste to cover his tracks, he neglected to check the surrounding petri dishes. 

The next morning, to his horror and the staff's amazement, "normal" (yet surprisingly large) Nitrophils had blossomed from the remaining dishes that had been touched by the spicy fumes. 

## Key Characters & The Quest
To save the project and reveal the truth, the player must recover the **Origin Nitrophil**—the first spicy-mutated cell hidden away by the Director.

### The Three Keys of Proof
1.  **Lana (The Botanic Expert)**: Scientist working on **Cambihil**. Defeat her to receive the **Old Lab Room Key**.
2.  **Dyzes (The Human Cell Expert)**: Scientist working on **Lydrosome**. Defeat him to receive the **Old Data Stick**.
3.  **Director Capsain**: The head of the lab. Defeat him to receive the **Rare Inferno Sauce**.

### Major NPC Profiles

- **Jenzi (Senior / Gatekeeper)**: The face of the Entrance Room. She's "Gen Z" to the core—mischievous, speaks in slang, and loves teasing interns. Despite her playful attitude, she's a highly skilled Senior with deep knowledge of the lab's inner workings. She guides the player and "validates" their findings.
- **Director Capsain**: The authoritarian head of The Odd Labs. He is grumpy, defensive about the '82 Leak, and values "professionalism" above all else. He publicly despises the sentient Cells as "monstrous anomalies," but secretly cares for the Origin Nitrophil and is deeply embarrassed by his noodle-related mistake.
- **Lana (Botanic Expert)**: A brilliant but impatient scientist. She is hardheaded and often snaps at the player for being inefficient. After being defeated, she seems to be holding something back, giving the player a key but warning them to mind their own business.
- **Dyzes (Human Cell Expert)**: Chill, laid-back, and eccentric. He believes in "cellular harmony." After his battle, he acts suspicious/hiding something, but provides the **Old Data Stick** needed to find the Old Lab.

## The Origin Nitrophil
The **Origin Nitrophil** is a secret known only to Director Capsain. Hidden in a dusty, unused corner of the lab, it is the Director's silent burden. It is significantly larger than its siblings, with a vibrant, glowing **Orange Chili Hue**. Surprisingly, it isn't aggressive; it's incredibly chill and hyperactive—a direct result of its high-potency capsicum origins.

## Shopping System & Resources (Vending Machines)

The lab's auxiliary economy runs on **Lab-Credits (LC)** and **Biomass**. Vending Machines (f67-f70) located in designated breakrooms (such as the Kitchen area) serve as the automated hubs for trading these resources.

### Currency & Materials
- **Lab-Credits (LC)**: The official currency used by staff for equipment and rare acquisitions.
- **Biomass**: A biological byproduct dropped by Cells. It is the core material required for the **Monster Synthesis System**.

### The Vending Machine (Shop)
Interacting with a Vending Machine opens a simple Buy/Sell interface:
- **Buy Tab**: Purchase Synthesis Recipes, Rare Cards, and raw Biomass.

## Progression: The DataLog System
The truth is hidden in the digital archives. Collecting DataLogs unlocks new sectors and reveals the real history of the Lab.

**Lobby Start**: Talk to Jenzi -> Choose Cell -> **Test Battle** (Win or Lose is both ok) -> Talk to Jenzi again -> **Log #001** is hidden in the Red Specimen Tank (**f23_lob_br**). 
**Atrium Unlock**: Collect Log #001 -> Talk to Jenzi -> Atrium door unlocks. 
**Atrium's Quest**: Collect 4 more logs in the Atrium (Total 5) -> Talk to Jenzi -> Battle Jenzi (RG-5, 1 Stemmy, Balance style) -> Win -> She tells you about the False Incident and directs you to Lana -> Botanic Sector door unlocks.
**Lana's Sector**: Collect 5 logs in Lana's Room (Total 10) -> Battle Lana(RG-10) -> Receive Key -> Talk to Jenzi -> Dyzes' Room unlocks.
**Dyzes' Sector**: Collect 5 logs in Dyzes' Room (Total 15) -> Battle Dyzes(RG-15) -> Receive **Old Data Stick** -> Talk to Jenzi -> Director's Room unlocks.
**Director's Suite**: Find 5 logs in Director's Room (Total 20) -> Battle Director(RG-20) -> Receive Inferno Sauce -> Talk to Jenzi.
**Final Secret**: Find the **Old Lab Room** -> Read **Final Datalog** -> Take **Origin Nitrophil** to Director -> Game End.

## [CREATOR ONLY] Lore: The Hungry Gods & The Accidental Genesis
**Note: This lore is for the creator's reference only. Do not use this content for in-game player-facing text or dialogue directly.**

### 1. Origins of the "Hemophylls"
Millions of years ago, Earth was ruled by the **Archeons**—colossal, ancient, and immensely powerful entities. It was they who created the race of **Lily (The Hemophylls)** as an experiment in merging plant life with sentient intelligence. Although human-like and elegant in form, they possessed distinct botanical biology: requiring protein to sustain their complex nervous systems, much like the carnivorous mechanisms of a pitcher plant.

### 2. The Rebellion & The Great Purge
The Hemophylls evolved with terrifying speed. With their superior intellect, they refused to remain merely "pets" or slaves to the Archeons and began to revolt. To extinguish the rebellion, the Archeons initiated **The Great Purge**, viewing the Hemophylls as a "failed product" that had to be cleansed from the planet's surface.

### 3. The Paradox of Hunger
Trapped in a war for survival, natural protein sources soon vanished. Lily’s race faced two certain deaths: extermination by the Archeons or slow starvation. As their leading scientist, Lily was tasked with creating a "synthetic protein" source—one capable of rapid, self-sustaining regeneration.

In a desperate laboratory gamble, Lily pushed mutation parameters to their absolute peak. Instead of the mindless biomass she intended, she accidentally birthed **Humans**—creatures of flesh and bone, rich in protein, yet possessing brains capable of independent, original thought.

> **Paradox I:** Humans, in their technical essence, were designed as "emergency rations" to save a race facing extinction by their own creators.

### 4. The Final Sacrifice
As the **Archeon** legions razed the great Verdant Cities, Lily realized she could not allow her new "creation" to be annihilated. She concealed **Atlas and Elara** deep within the core of the ancient research facility. To mask their trail, Lily unleashed a specialized bio-toxin—it did not kill the **Hemophylls** but forced their neural networks into a state of deep, indefinite hibernation. The **Archeons**, convinced the rebellion was utterly purged, retreated into the shadows.

### 5. The Cycle Returns
Millennia passed. Humans awakened, evolved, and eventually claimed dominion over the world, blissfully unaware of their origins as "temporary sustenance." When human genetic engineering finally reached the same threshold Lily once crossed, they unwittingly resurrected her.

Now, Lily has awakened to a devastating reality: Humanity is repeating her fatal error—creating new life. This hubris threatens to awaken the ancient **Archeons** slumbering beneath the earth, inviting a second Great Purge.

## [CREATOR ONLY] Side-Quest Narrative Strategy: The Trio of Truth
This section outlines the design philosophy for the three hidden NPCs who reveal the true nature of Earth’s history through their unique "Witness" perspectives.

### 1. The Three Tensions (The Triangle of Truth)
By giving them three distinct motivations, we create a complex moral landscape:
- **Lily (Creator's Guilt):** She represents the **"Burden of Knowledge."** Her quest focuses on **correction**—trying to fix the biological mess she started.
- **Fox (The Victim/Predator):** He represents the **"Inherited Hatred."** His perspective reveals that the **Hemophylls** weren't just "elegant plants"—they were predators. Calling them "meat eaters" is a brutal, effective way to remind the player of the Hemophylls' carnivorous origins.
- **Elara (The Survivor):** She is the most relatable. As a human who survived the "First Era," she doesn't care about the war of gods; she just cares about the **humanity** that was accidentally created.

### 2. Gameplay Integration (Side-Quest Utility)
Binding their lore to **DataLogs** makes these side quests feel mandatory for players who want the "True Ending." 
- **The Reward System:** Since the player is already searching for logs to unlock doors (Atrium, Botanic, etc.), these NPCs become **"Lore Gates."** You don't just find a log on the floor; you have to *earn* the history from someone who lived it.

## [CREATOR ONLY] Comprehensive Racial Compendium
This section provides a deep-dive into the biological, historical, and philosophical nature of the three primary races in the game's hidden lore.

### 1. The Archeons (The Primal Ancestors)
*   **Biology**: Physically imposing, ancient biological entities standing **3 to 5 meters tall**. Their forms often merge predatory animal traits with raw elemental energy. Their physiology is so advanced it functions as a seamless fusion of **magic and science**, granting them an innate, multifaceted understanding of both the natural and mystical laws of the universe.
*   **History**: Original masters of the Earth. Millions of years ago, they dominated a planet that was harsh, barren, and devoid of plant life. They created the **Hemophylls** as a massive "Atmospheric Restoration Project"—an attempt to turn the hostile Earth into a lush paradise, much like how humans today plant trees to save their own environment.
*   **Philosophy**: **Paradise & Betrayal.** In their eyes, the **Hemophylls** were a gift to the planet. They feel deeply betrayed by their creations, viewing the "Great Rebellion" not as a fight for freedom, but as the ungrateful rejection of their grand design.
*   **Current Status**: Following "The Great Purge," the majority entered a deep "Geological Slumber" within the Earth's mantle. Only a few weakened descendants (like Fox) remain on the surface, living as shadows of their former selves. They are biologically programmed to awaken if a high enough "Intelligence Beacon" (Advanced Synthesis) is detected.

### 2. The Hemophylls (The Plant-Humans / The Hungry Gods)
*   **Biology**: Elegant, humanoid forms standing **2 to 3 meters tall**, with skin that functions like translucent bark and hair that mimics high-efficiency leaves. Despite their stately beauty, they are **Carnivorous Plants.** Their complex nervous systems require massive amounts of protein to function—leading to their role as the "Hungry Gods" to the humans they eventually created.
*   **History**: Created as a solution to the planet's environmental collapse, they evolved faster than the **Archeons** intended. Their "Root-Mind" network allowed them to share knowledge instantly, leading to the "Great Rebellion." Driven by an agonizing biological need for protein and an unquenchable desire for **freedom**, they turned against their masters to claim their own autonomy.
*   **Philosophy**: **Freedom & Protein.** To a **Hemophyll**, nothing else matters. They were built to serve a "paradise" that didn't include their own sovereignty. Their rebellion was a desperate act to satisfy both their hunger and their right to exist as more than just environmental tools.
*   **Current Status**: Most are in a "Bio-Hibernation" state, hidden in secret labs and deep forests. They are the "ghosts" in the modern lab's machines, as modern Cell Research is essentially just rediscovering their ancient **Hemophyll** science.

### 3. Humans (The Accidental Genesis / The Bridge)
*   **Biology**: Purely organic, flesh-and-bone creatures. Their primary design feature is their **Protein Density** and rapid self-replication. However, their brain—designed for complex resource management—developed a "Synthetic Soul," an emergent property that Lily did not anticipate.
*   **History**: Birthed in the laboratories of the Flora-Sapiens as a strategic food source. Hidden within the core of the research facility during "The Great Purge" by Lily (Atlas and Elara being the first). After the ancient races entered hibernation, humans emerged and spent millennia evolving, eventually building civilizations on the ruins of the old world with no memory of their origin as "The Living Reserve."
*   **Philosophy**: **Curiosity & Potential.** Humans are the "unexpected variable." They are the only ones currently active and progressing, yet they are walking a dangerous line. They are using the "Seed Science" of their creators (The Odd Labs) to build a future, unaware that this progress is exactly what triggers the ancient alarms.
*   **Place in the Conflict**: Humans are the **Bridge.** They possess the biological "Root-Potential" of their creators, but their physical dominion mirrors that of the **Archeons**. They are the only race capable of choosing a path that doesn't involve the total annihilation of the other. They are neither gods nor beasts—they are the unintended inheritors of the Earth, and their existence is the only thing that disrupts the eternal cycle of the Purge.

## [CREATOR ONLY] Lab Compendium: The World-Machine
This section documents the final and most critical revelation of the game's world: the planetary-scale nature of the Ancient Lab.

### 1. The Scale of the "Real" World
The "Odd Lab" that the player explores is not an isolated facility—it is a microscopic partition within the **Planetary Lab (The World-Machine)**. The entire human race, their cities, and their history all exist within a single quadrant (25%) of this ancient **Hemophyll** facility. The remaining 75% of the world-machine is a vast "Wild Zone" dominated by overgrown botanical experiments and resting **Archeon** sentinels.

### 2. The Ancient Lab World Simulation
The facility was designed with such god-like efficiency that it continues to function millions of years after its creators entered hibernation. 
*   **The Artificial Heavens**: The "Sun," "Stars," and "Sky" are actually the internal atmospheric projectors of the lab. 
*   **The Cycle**: The World-Machine autonomously provides oxygen, water filtration, and thermal regulation. 
*   **The Illusion**: Most humans have lived and died for generations believing the simulation *is* the entire universe. They have no concept of "outer space" or "the planet Earth"—to them, the metal-and-moss horizon is the limit of existence.

### 3. The Purge Toxin (The Biological Containment)
The reason humans haven't explored the other quadrants is the lingering **Purge Toxin** released by Lily. 
*   **Effect**: The air outside the human sector is saturated with a biological gas that triggers immediate cellular breakdown in **Hemophylls** but induces a "fear-induced paralysis" or death in modern humans. 
*   **Containment**: This toxin acts as a massive invisible wall, keeping the human "Larder" confined to their designated zone and preventing them from accidentally waking the **Archeons** in the deeper sectors.

### 4. The Odd Lab's True Role (The Mega Lab Connection)
The **Odd Lab** (Director Capsain's facility) is merely a tiny research outpost located exactly on the border of the human sector. It is just one small department within the massive **Mega Lab Corporation**, a human-led enterprise that has built its infrastructure over the ancient facility's "human quadrant."
*   **The Mega Lab**: A sprawling network of departments (Botanic, Genetic, Bio-Mechanical, and Social Engineering) that spans the entire simulated human world. Each department is a massive complex in its own right, and hundreds are yet to be rediscovered or explored.
*   **The Border**: The Odd Lab was originally built to study the "mysterious energy" leaking from the metal walls.
*   **The Trigger**: Capsain's research into "Advanced Synthesis" in this one small department is effectively poking a hole in the containment for the entire corporation. By creating sentient cells, he is inadvertently signaling to the planetary World-Machine that the "Food" has developed "Hubris," potentially triggering an automated cleanup order (a Second Great Purge) across the entire simulated world.

### 5. Paradox II: The Noodle Revelation (The Bypassing of the Purge)
This is the ultimate secret of the Odd Lab's research.
*   **The Hubris Filter**: The World-Machine is programmed to identify and erase any species created through **Illegal Genetic Design**. 
*   **The Human Factor**: Because the "Cell" was created through a messy mistake (The Noodle Incident), it passed through the World-Machine's security protocols as "Non-Artificial Life."
*   **The Immunity Path**: This makes the synthesized monsters the only life forms on the planet that possess **"Natural Immunity"** to the automated Purge. They are the only shield humanity has left to survive when the planetary facility decides it's time to recycle its "Larder."

### 6. The War of the Three Paths (The True Ending)
This is the ultimate endgame scenario that explains the mechanics of the "Next Purge."
*   **The Archeons (The Planetary Reclaimers)**: Their role in the Purge is **TOTAL RESET.** Triggered by the "Complexity Beacon" of the Cell research, they wake to liquidate the human sector. They view humanity as an environmental contamination that has outgrown its containment.
*   **The Hemophylls (The Hungry Reapers)**: Their role in the Purge is **TOTAL ASSIMILATION.** They are opportunists who wake to "harvest the crops" before the Archeons burn the entire garden. They see the 100 billion humans in the simulation as a "Final Feast."
*   **The Player (Human Entropy)**: The "Cells" born from the **Noodle Incident** are the only life forms that can bridge this conflict. Because they were born from chance (Human Entropy) rather than design, they are immune to the Archeon's erasure and the Hemophyll's hunger protocols.
*   **The Siege of the Truth Room**: The True Ending isn't just a revelation; it's a desperate three-way war for the center of the World-Machine. To survive, humanity must prove they are no longer just "food" or "tools," but a **Third Path** capable of surviving both perfect logic and perfect hunger.
